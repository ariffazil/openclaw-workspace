## SECTION 3: STRATEGIC DECISIONS

### The Sarawak/Petros Surrender

This is arguably his most consequential and criticized decision.

**Background:**
- Sarawak asserted rights over domestic gas under its Distribution of Gas Ordinance (2022)
- PETRONAS' predecessor (Wan Zulkiflee) had been litigious — sued Kelantan to assert federal rights
- **Tengku Taufik chose conciliation over confrontation**
- Did not drag the dispute into open court
- By late 2022: PETRONAS quietly acceded — agreed in principle to cede domestic gas marketing to Petros
- May 2025: Formal Joint Declaration signed — PETRONAS concedes domestic gas aggregator role

**Strategic consequence:**
```
PETRONAS loses:  domestic gas marketing monopoly in Sarawak
PETRONAS gains:  "stabilized" relationship with Sarawak state
Long-term cost:  Revenue leakage to state-owned Petros; precedent set for Sabah
```

> *"Tengku Muhammad Taufik chose a different tack: conciliation over confrontation. He refrained from dragging the Sarawak dispute into open court."*

**Why this matters:** Federal-state dynamics in Malaysia mean this isn't just a corporate issue — it's a constitutional one. Once Sarawak gets gas autonomy, Sabah will follow. PETRONAS' territorial footprint in East Malaysia is permanently reduced under his watch.

### Gentari: Green Pivot or Greenwashing?

**What Gentari does:**
- Renewable energy subsidiary — solar, wind, hydrogen, EV fleets
- Green hydrogen production hub (targeting domestic use + export)
- Overseas expansion: India, Australia, Thailand, Southeast Asia
- Target: position as Asia Pacific renewable leader

**The criticism:**
- ESG branding vs. financial opacity — *"Gentari brand masks slow actual transition"*
- Most capacity growth is **overseas**, not domestic Malaysia
- CAPEX allocation to renewables not clearly disclosed
- Core business still fossil fuel — transition is gradual, not transformational

> *"A less rosy outlook: PETRONAS has been under pressure to trim costs and restructure."*
