## THE THIRD AXIS — WHAT ACEMOGLU MISSED

Acemoglu maps institutions on **two axes:**
- Political institutions (inclusive ↔ extractive)
- Economic institutions (inclusive ↔ extractive)

Two axes produce four quadrants. That's his entire model.

**But the model cannot explain this:**

A nation that nationalizes its oil should look extractive by Acemoglu's taxonomy. State controls the means of production. Workers don't own capital. Markets aren't perfectly competitive.

Yet Razak 1974 proved the opposite can be true. State ownership prevented **foreign extraction** — which is the most extractive arrangement of all. One colonizer's "inclusive institution" is another nation's extraction.

**The third axis is:**

```
FUNCTIONAL SOVEREIGNTY vs NOMINAL SOVEREIGNTY
```

The gap between who owns it on paper and who controls it in practice.

## THE THIRD AXIS DEFINED

**Resource Sovereignty** is a distinct structural dimension from inclusive/extractive institutions.

| Dimension | Question it answers |
|---|---|
| Political inclusivity | Who holds political power? |
| Economic inclusivity | Who participates in markets? |
| **Resource sovereignty** | **Who controls the actual resource?** |

A nation can have:
- Inclusive political institutions ✓
- Inclusive economic institutions ✓
- Nominal resource sovereignty ✓
- **Functional resource dependency** ✗ ← third axis collapsed

The inverse is also true:
- Nominal resource sovereignty may exist
- Functional sovereignty may be intact
- Even as economic institutions grow more extractive

## THREE TRAJECTORIES OF COLLAPSE

### TRAJECTORY A: Inclusivity breaks first (Acemoglu's map)

- Elites capture institutions
- Property rights narrow to connected firms
- Growth slows, then stops
- Regime relies on repression to maintain extraction
- **Signal:** independent courts captured, regulatory barriers rising, new entry blocked
- **Examples:** USSR 1991, Venezuela mid-2010s, Argentina century-long decline

### TRAJECTORY B: Resource Sovereignty breaks first (Arif's map)

- NOC nominal ownership remains — state still owns resource on paper
- Operational control leaks to: international partners in "joint ventures," technical partners who hold know-how, financial institutions with debt covenants
- Planning horizon collapses under crisis pressure (90 days → 45 days)
- Capital allocation shifts from strategic to operational survival
- No long-term infrastructure investment, no human capital pipeline, no R&D
- Nation has nominal sovereignty but **functional dependency**
- **Signal:** NOC starts talking about efficiency instead of capacity, planning horizons compress, domestic supply projections shorten
- **Examples:** (pre-collapse indicators visible in) Malaysia 2026, potential future Indonesia NOC scenarios

### TRAJECTORY C: Both axes collapse simultaneously

- Most dangerous configuration
- Never had real resource sovereignty (relied on foreign NOCs for technical capability)
- Loses political inclusivity at same time
- **Examples:** Libya 2011, Iraq 2003, Syria 2011
