## 3. PETROS — What It Is and Why It Matters

### 3.1 What Is PETROS?

**PETROS** = Petroleum Sarawak Berhad
- State-owned petroleum company representing Sarawak's interest in petroleum resources
- Established to be the single aggregator for all gas in Sarawak
- Sarawak's position: PETROS should be the sole buyer and aggregator of all petroleum produced in Sarawak

### 3.2 The Core Dispute

| PETRONAS Position | PETROS/SARAWAK Position |
|-------------------|------------------------|
| PETRONAS has federal mandate over Malaysia's petroleum | Sarawak's petroleum belongs to Sarawak under the Malaysia Agreement 1963 (MA63) |
| PETRONAS Carigali (or its JVs) can hold operatorship | PETROS should be sole aggregator — all gas must be sold to PETROS first |
| Gas from Sarawak can flow to PENINSULAR Malaysia at PETRONAS-set prices | Sarawak wants control over pricing and allocation for Sarawak's own use |

### 3.3 Timeline of the Dispute

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1963 | Malaysia Agreement — Sabah and Sarawak join Malaysia. Extent of petroleum rights disputed. |
| 1974 | Petroleum Development Act — gives PETRONAS control over all petroleum in Malaysia. Sarawak not fully consulted. |
| 1990s-2000s | Sarawak begins push for more petroleum rights. |
| 2016 | PETROS officially launched — Sarawak's move to reclaim control. |
| 2020 | Operatorship of gas distribution assets in Miri and Bintulu transferred to PETROS. Long delayed. |
| 2024 | Negotiations for a full gas aggregation agreement between PETRONAS and PETROS intensifying. End-2025 target set. |
| Dec 2025 | Expected conclusion of PETRONAS-PETROS gas agreement. **Not concluded.** |
| Feb 2026 | SEARA Energy Limited incorporated (UK) — Malaysian assets potentially affected. |
| April 2026 | SEARAH JV publicly announced. PETRONAS-PETROS negotiations STILL unresolved. |
