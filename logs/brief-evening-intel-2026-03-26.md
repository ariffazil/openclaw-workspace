🌙 Evening Synthesis — March 26, 2026

Today's Big Moves: What happened while you were away
• The Strait of Hormuz closure continues to rattle global markets. With 20% of the world's oil and LNG supply disrupted, WTI crude is now pushing toward the $98 per barrel mark.
• The energy shock is starting to ripple through the broader economy, raising immediate concerns about a resurgence in inflation and depleting foreign currency reserves for importing nations. 

AI & Tech Pulse: Tools and trends worth knowing
• The conversation around AI capabilities is splitting. The latest industry reports (like the Anthropic/TechCrunch analysis) are highlighting a widening "skills gap." The divide is no longer just about who uses AI, but who is using it casually versus who is acting as a "meta-architect" to build robust governance systems around it.
• On the agentic front, independent researchers are pushing back on vendor safety claims. While some companies self-report high success rates at blocking prompt injections, new studies suggest that adaptive, real-world attacks on coding and browser agents are still highly effective. 

Tomorrow's Watch: Keep an eye on these
1. Energy Transit Updates: Any news on the duration of the Hormuz disruption. The longer this lasts, the more severe the LNG and oil pricing impacts will be for Europe and Asia.
2. Central Bank Chatter: Watch for any emergency signaling from the Fed or other central banks regarding how they plan to handle the sudden inflationary pressure from the energy spike.
3. Agentic Security Patches: Keep an eye out for new defensive tools. As the industry admits that browser and coding agents remain vulnerable to prompt injection, we're seeing a shift toward using automated "LLM attackers" to constantly red-team systems.

So What?: The bottom line
The geopolitical energy shock means macro volatility is back in a big way, which will likely constrain venture and tech spending. At the same time, the realization that agentic AI cannot be perfectly secured by the models themselves validates our approach of building hard, systemic constraints and veto gates rather than relying purely on prompt-level safety.
