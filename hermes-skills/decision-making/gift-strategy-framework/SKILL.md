# Gift Strategy Framework

## When to use
When someone asks you to help choose a gift AND there's a tension between:
- The giver's stated values (e.g., "no diminishing economics value")
- The recipient's expressed preference (e.g., "I want X instead")

## Trigger conditions
- Someone asks for gift recommendations for a specific person
- Recipient has a life transition (new job, graduation, farewell)
- Recipient self-selects a "comfort" gift when a "survival" gift is more appropriate
- The giver has explicit value constraints on what makes a good gift

## The Framework

### Step 1: Build the Decision Matrix
Map each candidate against three axes:

| Axis | What it measures |
|------|-----------------|
| Gift durability | Re-read value, keepsake, multi-decade reference |
| Practical utility | Direct career/life application |
| Recipient fit | Does it match who they are, not just what they want right now? |

### Step 2: Identify the giver's stated values
These are NON-NEGOTIABLE constraints. Common examples:
- "I don't like giving things that diminish in value"
- "It must have practical use"
- "I want them to actually read it"
- "Economics matter more than comfort"

### Step 3: The Critical Question Test
Before deciding, ask the recipient a diagnostic question:

> *"You felt [X] strongly. Then you said [Y]. Are you choosing comfort right now — or do you actually want [Y] for a reason that matters?"*

The answer reveals whether they're:
- **Retreating** → redirect to the practical option
- **Genuinely self-aware** → respect their choice
- **Wanting both** → layer the gifts

### Step 4: The Layering Override
When the tension is comfort vs. practical:
> *"I'll get you both. The cat book for when you're tired. Greene for when you're ready."*

This satisfies the giver's economic constraint (both are kept) while respecting the recipient's autonomy.

### Step 5: For self-selected children's/literature comfort books specifically
Check Goodreads before recommending:
- Rating below 4.0 = weak signal
- Book 2 of a series = usually a mistake (need Book 1 first)
- Children's lit archetype = almost never survives re-gifting
- Published within 1 year = too new to know staying power

## Key insight
The counterintuitive gift principle: **redirecting someone from their comfort choice to a practical one when they're in a vulnerable transition is an act of care, not imposition.** The giver's values + the recipient's actual needs > the recipient's expressed preference in transitional moments.

## Variables that change the answer
- Recipient's financial independence (if they can buy it themselves, autonomy wins)
- Timing of the gift (farewell vs. random Tuesday — farewell gifts should be durable)
- Life transition type (comfort-first is valid for emotional transitions, wrong for career transitions)
